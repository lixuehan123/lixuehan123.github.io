Project Overview

This is a Hades-like Roguelike team project developed for the Dota 2 Arcade. This page presents my design thinking for an earlier version of the build system. I began with how players actually operate each hero, then broke blessings into trigger conditions, benefits, costs, and cross-system interfaces so a hero's control pattern could strengthen its affinity with particular builds. The system supports beginners who focus on one blessing family while preserving cross-system combinations, deliberate risk-taking, and higher ceilings for experienced players.

My goal was not a collection of isolated numerical upgrades, but a build network that could serve different heroes, control styles, and levels of player understanding. Every blessing acts as an entry condition, benefit multiplier, weakness compensator, or cost. Beginners can form clear but capped internal loops within one system; experienced players can connect conditions across systems and exchange higher execution demands, risk, or assembly difficulty for a higher ceiling. These build paths also test whether hero skills provide meaningful entry points.

Four Core Principles

1

Define Action Entry Points and Hero Affinity First

A hero's attack rhythm, movement frequency, defensive behavior, and skill links determine its natural build tendencies. I first identify which actions players can execute reliably, how often they occur, and which systems they can connect to, then design the corresponding benefits and values.

2

Create Curved Growth Through Conditions

If players need movement speed, I do not necessarily grant a flat speed bonus. They might first enter a lightning status, then use other blessings to convert that condition into speed, damage, or survivability. Players search for an activation condition instead of only comparing numbers.

3

Match the Ceiling to Risk, Execution, and Assembly Difficulty

Builds that are harder to execute, harder to assemble, or require intentional health loss should offer higher potential returns. Low-risk builds emphasize stability and should not also have the highest ceiling.

4

Preserve Single-System Loops for Beginners

Beginners often keep selecting one blessing family. Each major system therefore contains a usable internal build, but its strength remains below advanced combinations that require recognizing keywords and connecting multiple systems.

Three Player Layers

Accessible

Beginner: Single-System Completion

Repeatedly selecting one god or blessing family still produces a basic loop of triggers, enhancement, and survival. The build remains functional without deep system knowledge, but its ceiling is controlled.

Combinational

Advanced: Cross-System Assembly

Players follow keywords such as critical chance, movement speed, status effects, summons, or cooldowns to find starters, multipliers, and stabilizers across several systems, creating a clearer gameplay identity.

Mastery

Expert: High Risk, High Ceiling

Players deliberately accept low health, self-damage, long cooldowns, stationary play, or high execution demands, using a more complex assembly path to gain stronger damage and combat rhythm.

Critical Build

Players can seek direct critical chance, guaranteed criticals after dashing, a guaranteed critical every few attacks, armor-break conversion, critical multipliers, or critical-to-true-damage conversion. The goal is not to stack one number, but to improve both trigger reliability and the value of each trigger.

Low-Health / Damage-Taken Build

Reduced maximum health, continuous health loss, and on-damage triggers provide strong returns, while healing, mitigation, revive counts, and per-hit damage caps manage the risk. The build must solve both how to enter danger and how to remain there without dying immediately.

Dash Build

How Builds Feed Back into Hero Skills

After establishing the build network, I check whether each hero has sufficiently clear starting points. If a build works only after a lucky blessing drop, the hero's skills need a stable but incomplete base condition. This lets players identify a direction early while still requiring later choices to amplify it.

Hero Control Traits
Create Build Needs
Identify Missing Conditions
Adjust Hero Skill Entry Points
  • Timing-Based Criticals: A correctly timed charged basic attack always crits, making execution precision a stable entry point for critical builds.
  • Continuous Movement: The hero gradually gains temporary movement speed while moving and loses it when stopping, making movement behavior part of the build loop.
  • Lightning Summon: The hero can summon a lightning creature, creating a natural interface between lightning and summon systems.
