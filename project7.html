Project Introduction

Petalnaut is an academic team-developed top-down shooter-puzzle that combines room-based puzzles inspired by classic 2D Zelda with the top-down shooting of Enter the Gungeon. As level designer, I built Level 1 and the final level. Level 1 gradually establishes the rules for character abilities, enemies, and environmental hazards; the final level asks players to combine those mechanics and make more complex spatial decisions under combat pressure.

My process begins by breaking the character's abilities into reusable puzzle blocks. I then recombine enemy placement, hazard zones, ability order, and room structure to create new puzzles and combat rhythms. This preserves consistent rules while producing new decisions from familiar systems.

My Work

  • Level 1: Introduced the core mechanics so players could understand character abilities, enemy attacks, and room objectives in a low-pressure environment.
  • Final Level: Recombined previously learned mechanics into one sequence of combat, puzzles, and ability switching.
  • Puzzle Blocks: Designed independent puzzle modules for different protagonist abilities, then expanded solutions through arrangement, overlap, and sequencing.
  • Low-Cost Content Expansion: Reused completed mechanics and code to assemble new enemies and traps, reducing additional programming needs.

Design Analysis

Building Puzzle Blocks from One Ability

Each puzzle block begins with one clear ability and one fundamental problem: crossing a hazard, changing position, handling a ranged threat, or reaching a goal under constraints. A module emphasizes only a few rules so players understand why an action succeeds or fails.

Moving from Teaching to Combined Use

Level 1 introduces abilities, enemies, and environmental rules one at a time. The final level stops repeating isolated lessons and places several puzzle blocks in the same room or sequence. Players must determine the order of operations and switch abilities while managing projectile pressure, area attacks, and terrain hazards.

Expanding Level Content by Reusing Systems

I first ask what new combinations the existing mechanics can support before requesting new code. By adjusting attack range, movement, enemy composition, and environmental conditions, I can build new enemies and traps from existing systems and reserve team resources for more critical features.
